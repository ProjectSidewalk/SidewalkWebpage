Health #

Database and application operational-health signals at a glance, so problems that don't surface in the app log — a lock held by an abandoned transaction, a stuck evolution, autovacuum falling behind, connection-pool pressure — are visible without a manual log dig. Signals are cluster-wide across all city databases. This page refreshes itself every few seconds.

Blocking locks #

Sessions that are currently blocking one or more others from acquiring a lock. A session that holds a strong lock (for example on street_edge) while idle in a transaction can silently stall every reader of that table. Ordered worst-first.

Idle transactions #

Sessions sitting in an open transaction while idle. A leaked transaction that never commits or rolls back holds its locks the whole time, so long-lived rows here are worth investigating. Oldest first.

Stuck evolutions #

Play evolutions that are mid-apply or carry a recorded problem, across every city schema. A failed evolution keeps a city's app instance from coming up cleanly.

Table bloat & autovacuum #

Dead-tuple bloat and last-vacuum age for the heavyweight tables (street_edge, label, audit_task_interaction) across cities. High bloat with a stale last-vacuum means autovacuum is falling behind — often because it can't get a lock.

DB connections #

Client-backend connections grouped by role and state. Each city's app instance has its own pool ceiling, so a role whose active connections approach that ceiling is under pressure.

Pano downloads #

Backup-image coverage for this city's labeled panos. The viewer shows a locally-hosted backup when one exists and otherwise falls back to the live source, so a labeled pano whose source has expired and has no backup can no longer be shown.
